A Novel Concept of Marine Biology Studies

The Context:
While increasing amounts of capital have been invested in marine and biomedical research, fewer and fewer innovations are making their way from the lab into useful products and therapeutics. 
Part of the reason for this is that few places exist that can train future leaders in the skills required to translate basic research into tomorrow’s breakthrough products. Academic institutions are primarily focused on asking very basic questions about how the universe operates. Basic research is more speculative and takes a long time – often measured in decades – to be applied in any practical context. Industry, polytechnics and technology institutes are typically more focused on applied research. Applied research is characterized as being capable of having an impact in practice within a relatively short time-frame, but would often represent an incremental improvement to current processes rather than delivering radical breakthroughs.
Translational research by contrast is a paradigm for research alternative to the dichotomy of basic research and applied research. It is often applied in the domain of translational medicine but has more general applicability as a distinct research approach. Translational scientists are trained to analyze the practical implications of their research, and to shorten the time-frame that fundamental research results in practical applications. Translational research is a much more iterative style of research with low and permeable barriers and a great deal of interaction between academic research and industry practice. Practitioners help shape the research agenda in supplying what may be intractable problems to which applied research approaches will only offer incremental improvements.
Currently, the cultural and physical divide that exists between basic and applied research institutions makes it difficult to establish the multidisciplinary and multi-skilled teams that are necessary to be successful in translational research. Other challenges arise in the traditional incentives which reward individual principal investigators over the types of multi-disciplinary teams that are necessary for translational research.
While there are small centers for translational research in Israel, and small basic research facilities focused on the marine environment, a translational research institute focused on marine science  does not exist anywhere in the world.
